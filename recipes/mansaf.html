Mansaf

Description

Mansaf is the national dish of Jordan, a celebratory meal made with tender lamb cooked in fermented dried yogurt sauce and served over a bed of rice and flatbread.

This rich and flavorful dish is traditionally enjoyed communally and is a symbol of Jordanian hospitality and culture.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs lamb (bone-in pieces)
  • 2 cups jameed or laban (dried yogurt or plain yogurt)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 2 tbsp ghee or clarified butter
  • 1/2 cup almonds or pine nuts (toasted)
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • Salt and pepper
  • Flatbread (shrak or markook)

Steps

  1. Rinse and soak the rice for 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, saut√© onions in ghee, then add lamb pieces and brown on all sides.
  3. Add water and simmer the lamb for 1.5 to 2 hours until tender.
  4. In a separate pot, dissolve jameed in warm water or use laban/yogurt and bring to a simmer.
  5. Combine the yogurt mixture with the lamb broth and simmer together for 20 minutes.
  6. Cook the rice with turmeric, salt, and a bit of ghee.
  7. Toast almonds or pine nuts in ghee until golden.
  8. Place flatbread on a large platter, then spread the rice over it.
  9. Top with the lamb pieces and pour the yogurt sauce generously over the top.
  10. Garnish with toasted nuts and serve warm.
